Chem 260: Thermodynamics, Equilibria, and Kinetics
Class Schedule

The schedule below outlines each day's main topic(s) and lists reading assigments drawn from Chemistry by OpenStax College.

Unit I: Thermodynamics

In which we develop an understanding of why some reactions are favorable and other reactions are unfavorable, and how to predict which reactions are favorable.

  • August 24: no class (away at conference)
  • August 26: introduction to course; skim Chapter 4 to review stoichiometry
  • August 29: review of chemical reactions and introduction to thermodynamics
  • August 31: measuring the flow of heat in a chemical reaction
  • September 2: a first look at enthalpy - heats of reaction
  • September 5: a second look at enthalpy - atom combinations and the importance of state functions
  • September 7: a third look at enthalpy - heats of formation
  • September 9: where did all the heat go - entropy
  • September 12: what makes a reaction favorable - free energy
  • September 14: driving unfavorable reactions
  • September 16: thermodynamics of redox reactions
  • September 19: reaction dynamics
  • September 21: first exam

Unit II: Equilibrium Chemistry

In which we learn to predict the composition of a system when it reaches a state of equilibrium and apply this to acid-base reactions, solubility reactions, and metal-ligand complexation reactions.

  • September 23: free energy and equilbria
  • September 26: kinetics and equilibria
  • September 28: DePauw Dialogue
  • September 30: general approaches to solving equilibrium problems
  • October 3: disturbing a reaction at equilibrium
  • October 5: monoprotic weak acids and weak bases
  • October 7: freactional abundance of weak acids and weak bases
  • October 10: polyprotic weak acids and weak bases
  • October 12: introduction to buffers
  • October 14: how buffers work
  • October 17: Fall Break
  • October 19: Fall Break
  • October 21: Fall Break
  • October 24: designing a buffer
  • October 26: solubility equilbria
  • October 28: controlling solubility using common ions and pH
  • October 31: controlling solubility using complexation reactions
  • November 2: why equilibrium calculations often do no agree with experimental results
  • November 4: second exam

Unit III: Kinetics of Chemical Reactions

In which we explore how a reaction's progress changes over time and what information that conveys about the reaction at a microscopic level.

  • November 7: introduction to kinetics
  • November 9: rate laws for chemical reactions
  • November 11: how to study kinetics in the laboratory - pseudo-order experiments
  • November 14: how to study kinetics in the labroatory - initial rate method
  • November 16: kinetics of reactions with multiple pathways
  • November 18: reaction mechanisms and activation energy
  • November 21: thermodynamics and kinetics

Unit IV: Wrapping Up

In which we revisit the topics of Units I, II, and III, reviewing what we covered and extending our understanding of the relationships between thermodynamics, equilibrium chemistry, and kinetics.

  • December 5: review of thermodynamics
  • December 7: review of equilibrium chemistry
